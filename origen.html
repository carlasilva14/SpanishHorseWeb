PHILIPS II DREAM

Philip II of Spain dedicated 8,000 ducats to the construction of the Royal Stables and commissioned their operation. The commission consisted of building stables to house the stallions, purchasing or leasing pastureland in the Guadalquivir Valley where the mares would graze and breed, and—most importantly—selecting and purchasing, according to his criteria, the best mares and stallions from those lands to carry out a process of selection and improvement of the breed. This Royal Stud Farm of Córdoba was the origin of the Andalusian horse breed.

THE ROYAL STABLES

This entire process unfolded during the Renaissance, a time when equestrian shows became fashionable. Added to this was the health benefits that physicians attributed to horseback riding and games with horses, making equestrianism a very common practice among the aristocracy. This new reality led to the need to find a breed that didn't yet exist: a beautiful and agile horse, a far cry from the medieval horses used for fighting and transport, which lacked any regard for their aesthetics.

SOME MORE HISTORY

Throughout its five centuries of history, the breeding of the Purebred Spanish Horse has gone through different periods, some critical, such as the French invasion at the beginning of the 19th century or the Industrial Revolution, which disrupted its role in agriculture. However, King Alfonso XIII marked a milestone with the establishment of a registry for the Spanish horse.

THE CIVIL WAR

Later, during the Civil War, the species faced difficult years, but the sector gradually recovered. First, with the implementation of the European common market, Spain became the third most important tourist destination.

EVOLUTION

For over 30 years, the breed was refined in the Caliphate city, giving rise to an improved breed and what was considered the perfect horse. This equine breed became an emblem of the Spanish Empire and was initially intended for the exclusive use of the Royal Household. The Spanish horse has evolved to unimaginable levels; its extraordinary qualities have been adapted to market demand, preserving above all its essence, which has allowed for an optimistic outlook on the world of high-level competition.
